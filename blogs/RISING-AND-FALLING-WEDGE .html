RISING AND FALLING WEDGE CHART PATTERN

Wedges are similar to symmetrical triangle patters. The trading takes place initially over wide price range and then reduce as the trading continues. It is hard to identify these patterns in the chart since they look similar to the background trading activity.

RISING CHART PATTERN (Ascending wedge)

The rising wedge chart pattern is a visible price move formed when the market strengthens between two converging support and resistance lines. The support and resistance lines must point in upward direction to form a rising wedge. The support line must be steeper than the resistance.

Wedges often lead to breakouts similar to head and shoulder, triangle and flags. It is often bearish.

  • When a market is at rise, it’s an indication that traders are thinking for bull move.
  • When a market falls there’s a short period of pause before the bear market takes hold.

Derivative analysis is the financial instruments that are obtained by the primary assets. For example- currencies, commodities, stocks, index, stocks or interest values. It usually involves risks and potential rewards of trading in financial instruments. These analysis is carried on by analyzing the past price movements, and unpredictability, and different other conditions to forecast the derivatives performancesInitially, ascending wedge resembles a bullish move because each successive peak and trough is higher than the last peak. It is important to note that the upward move shortens each time which indicates the formation of bearish opinion.

As the market goes beyond the trendline, it builds up the negative emotion among traders, prompting those who are in long positions to quickly close their trades to minimize the losses. And the one who waits to shorten the market jumps in. This causes a wave of selling stocks that result in significant downward momentum.

Ascending wedges is popular among technical traders occurring in the market that includes, Indices, Forex, and stocks.

WHAT IS FALLING WEDGE CHART PATTERN (DESCENDING WEDGE)

The Falling wedge can be either reversal or continuation signal. It is recognizable price move that is formed when a market integrates between the intersection of support and resistance lines. It is often bullish. The support and resistance lines must move in downward direction and the resistance line has to be deeper than line of support to form a descending wedge.

As the name suggests, the falling wedge is opposite of a rising wedge. It often leads to breakouts. Ascending wedges lead to bearish moves and descending pattern lead to bullish moves.

  • When the market upsurge, the falling wedge displays a short term pause before the long term move that takes hold once again.
  • The traders rethink in the bear move when the market is falling.

In contrast to, it may seem that the falling market as a sign of bull move. Mostly importantly note that the falling wedge gets shorter and shorter. Here, it’s clear indication that the bullish opinion is either formed or reformed. According to the studies, it is evident that the falling wedges lead to breakout more slightly than the rising ones. You’ll need a platform such as Meta Trader 4 or other related softwares to spot them.

WHAT ARE THE BEST STRATEGIES TO TRADE WEDGE PATTERNS

There are few factors to determine while making strategies in wedge patters. Below are the best strategies to be followed to trade wedge patterns.

  • BREAKOUT TRADING STRATEGY

    • The traders use breakout trading strategy as a signal to start a trade. As the price breaks below the lower trendline, the traders enter into short positions in wedge rising formation. Likewise, when the price breaks above the higher tendline the traders enter into a long position.

  • RETRACEMENT TRADING STRATEGY:

    • Traders use retracement trading strategy to enter a trade. The trader gets a fair chance to enter a short position at a better price when the price revokes at the lower trendline.

  • CONTINUATION TRADING STRATEGY:

    • The traders use breakout trading strategy as a signal to start a trade. As the price breaks below the lower trendline, the traders enter into short positions in wedge rising formation. Likewise, when the price breaks above the higher tendline the traders enter into a long position.The trader uses this strategy when they is an indication of continuous current trend. Traders engage in long position by using the breakout point and go on with trading in uptrend if a wedge pattern rises during an uptrend.

  • MOMENTUM TRADING STRATEGY:

    • The process of purchasing and selling assets depending upon the most recent price movements with a hope that this movements moves continuously in the same direction is said to be momentum trading strategy. In this strategy the trader look for the possibilities to trade when the asset’s price breaks out of patterns in wedge trading strategy.

    One of the success mantra of any trading strategy depends upon the trader’s proficiency in technical analysis, experience, risk management skills, and efficiency to take trading decisions.

HOW TO TRADE THE WEDGE STRATEGY

It has a signal line.The signal line is either the upper or the lower line pattern depending on the wedge type. Consider the following example:

The signal line is in the lower level when you have a rising wedge that connects the bottom of the wedge .The signal line is in the upper level when you have a falling wedge that connects the formation’s tops.The simple rule of wedge strategy- As you see the break in the signal line, you should join the market in the direction of the break.

For instance, when you have rising wedge, the signal line is in the lesser level of the figure. You should go short, when you find the equity breaking the wedge’s lower level. Simultaneously, when you get falling wedge, when the price breaks the upper level of the formation you should enter the market.

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN RISING AND FALLING WEDGE STRATEGIES:

  • Rising wedge are made when support and resistance lines meet upwards, whereas in the falling wedge the support and resistance lines meet downwards.
  • The support line is deeper than resistance in rising wedge, whereas in falling wedge the support line should be deeper than support.
