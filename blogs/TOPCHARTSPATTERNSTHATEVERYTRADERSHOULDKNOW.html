TOP CHARTS PATTERNS THAT EVERY TRADER SHOULD KNOW

TOP CHARTS PATTERNS THAT EVERY TRADER SHOULD KNOW

Chart patterns are essential details of technical analysis require knowledge before they can be used effectively. In general, traders use price patterns and stocks charts to provide technical traders with valuable insights of stock market in order to take good trading decisions. Previously we have discussed some of the chart patterns. Now, let’s learn more about different chart patterns below:

  • Head and Shoulders
  • Double Top
  • Double Bottom
  • Rounding Bottom
  • Cup and Handle
  • Pennant or Flags

Every chart pattern is used to highlight different trends in a market. Candle stick trading shows the simple representation of the opens and closes of the market. Some patterns are more suitable for a volatile market. Likewise, some patterns are best suited in bullish market and others in bearish market. It’s significant to know the best chart patterns for your specific market, as using the incorrect one or uninformed may cause you to miss out an opportunity to earn the returns.

HEAD AND SHOULDERS

Head and shoulders is a chart pattern with a large peak which has minute smaller peak on the either side. Traders look and decide and predict a bullish to bearish reversal. Normally, the second peak is higher than the first and the third peak, but they all fall on same support. This support is called as ‘neckline’. As soon as the third peak falls back to the level of support, it’s likely that it will breakdown into a bearish downtrend.

DOUBLE TOP

A Double top is next pattern that traders utilize to highlight trend reversals. Frequently, an asset’s price will reach a peak, before retreating back to a level of support. And then it climbs up again before reversing back permanently against the prevailing trend.

DOUBLE BOTTOM

A double bottom chart pattern indicates a period of selling,that causes an asset’s price to drop below support level. later, it rises again to a level of resistance, before declining again. Eventually, the trend reverses and signaling an upward motion as the market becomes more bullish. The double bottom chart pattern is a bullish reversal pattern, because it implies the end of a downtrend and a shift at an uptrend.

ROUNDING BOTTOM

A rounding bottom indicates a either continuation or a reversal in the trend. For example, the asset’s price may fall to certain extend before rising once more during an uptrend. This could be a bullish continuation. Traders seek to capitalize on this pattern by purchasing in midst around the bottom, at the low point, and capitalizing on the continuation and at one point it breaks above a level of resistance.

CUP AND HANDLE

The Cup and Handle pattern is a bullish continuation pattern that depicts a period of bearish market sentiment before the whole trend continues finally in a bullish motion. The cup looks similar to a rounding bottom chart pattern, and wedge pattern as the handle. Consequently the rounding bottom, the price of an asset will probably enter a temporary retracement; it is known as the handle due to this retracement that is restrained to two parallel lines on the price graph. The asset will finally reverse out of the handle and carry on with the bullish trend.

PENNANT OR FLAGS

In Pennant patterns or flags, it’s created after an asset undergoes a period of upward movement, followed by a consolidation. Typically, they will be an increase during the early stages of the trend, before it enters into a line of smaller upward and downward movements. Pennant represents a continuation or a reversal pattern that can be pretty bullish or bearish. A Pennant chart pattern can be identical to a wedge pattern or a triangle pattern. It’s bilateral continuation of pattern. It’s evident that wedges are narrower than pennants or triangles. Wedges are different from pennants because a wedge is ascending or descending, whereas a pennant is always parallel.

CONCLUSION

All the patterns explained above are helpful in technical indicators which help traders to know how or why an asset’s price moved in a specific range to which way, it might move in the future. It is due to the patterns that are capable of emphasizing areas of support and resistance, which can help a trader decide whether they should open a long or short position. Or to close out their open positions in the event of a possible trend reversal.
