RISK MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

RISK MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

Risk is inevitable in finance sector. It is defined as the process of identifying, evaluating, and alleviates losses in the investment. The trends are formed by the trader’s emotions that can be effected by many factors. Primarily these factors are elections, economic events like interest rate decisions, and business events for example new and innovative technologies, company performance, among others. These factors determine the profits and at the same time they can expose traders to various risks. To handle these situations and take right decisions, we must understand the basics and importance, and strategies of risk management.

STRATEGIES IN THE STOCK MARKET

It is a known fact that the strategy depends on the type of risk. You need to understand that each risk is different that can effect on your organization, therefore one should use right strategy to treat them.

Some of the common strategies in the stock market are as follows:

Regulating risk tolerance

Risk tolerance is the amount of finance that you are willing to lose at the time of trading or investment. It is a significant part of risk management on which many factors are depended. It includes financial situation, investment goals, time factor, and personality. By knowing the risk tolerance, one can set realistic and accomplishable goals and avoid taking excessive or unnecessary risks.

DIVERSIFICATION OF ASSETS

Diversification in a trading strategy functions as a risk management approach that includes dispersing investments among different asset securities. The main purpose is to lower the influence of market fluctuations on the portfolio. It helps to lower the overall risk of the portfolio by reducing the connections between the returns of different securities. For instance, if one invest in both stocks and bonds, the loss incurred in one asset maybe neutralized by the profits in another. Portfolio diversifications can also be captured for the benefits of various market segments and opportunities.

HEDGING INVESTMENTS

Hedging is a strategy to cut down the potential losses where financial tools or futures contracts are used. This investment is used in case the market goes against the expectations of the traders. This method protects the portfolio value by fixing specific price or return for the fundamental securities. For Example: if anyone anticipates that a stock’s price drops, they can buy a put option, allowing them the ability to sell the stock at set price.

Placing stop-loss and take-profit order

A stop loss is defined as to sell a security if it reaches the specific price or percentage below the buying price. In contrast to, a take profit order is used to sell a security touches a certain price or percentage above the buying price. These orders are predominant choice among risk management in trading. They help to facilitate the trading process and prevent emotional instability. It helps to control the limit losses and lock in profits of a trade.

Consider the following example, if one purchases a stock at Rs 1000 and sets-loss order at Rs 900 and a take-profit order at Rs1100, The stock will be automatically sold if it reaches either of these prices, Thus making sure a maximum loss of Rs 100 or a maximum profit of Rs100 per share.

REGULATING AND REVIEWING THE PORTFOLIO:

It is important to regulate and review the performance and risk to mark the mandatory adjustments with effect to the changing market conditions and goals. This process help to identify the strengths and weakness of the portfolio, analyze the effectiveness of the risk management strategies, and take relative actions if needed. For instance: If the portfolio is underperforming or overexposed to a risk, there is an option where one can balance the portfolio by selling some of underperforming or risky securities. One can purchase few better-performance or less risky securities for better risk management in trading

FEW POINTS TO PONDER FOR EFFECTIVE RISK MANAGEMENT IN TRADING:

DOLLAR COST AVERAGING

Dollar-cost averaging is the fixed amount of money is invested on regular basis in an organization, irrespective of market conditions.

Fundamental Evaluation

The evaluation helps in determining a company’s true value by examining its financial conditions, industry trends, and other informative data. The purpose is to locate stocks that are undervalued and have a potential for growth.

Summary

Risk management is a vital skill for any investor who desires to succeed in the stock market. By using the above strategies in risk management, one can gain the competitive edge in the market and achieve their financial goals.
