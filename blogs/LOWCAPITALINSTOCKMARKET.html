HOW CAN I INVEST WITH LOW CAPITAL IN STOCK MARKET

HOW CAN I INVEST WITH LOW CAPITAL IN STOCK MARKET

One of the common believes of many novice investors is whether they can invest in the market with low capital. These misunderstandings prevent the beginners to enjoy the benefits of starting early and growing their wealth. this helps the investors to save finances to invest in future shares.But the good point is that the investor can start their investment mere Rs 100 in the stock market and enjoy the generated wealth in long run.

Below are few points that the investor must consider to invest a little in the stock market:

ACHKNOWLEDGE YOUR FINANCES:

Understand the purpose of investment. The main objectives can be retirement, or intent to purchase a house, or maybe children’s education or marriage, travelling etc. Based on the goals, you can define the time length and the type of return you need.

VALUATION OF YOUR RISK TOLEREANCE

The valuation helps you to select the right kind of stocks. Try to determine risk which you can identify with:

  1. High risk
  2. Medium risk
  3. Low risk
  4. Medium high risk
  5. Medium low risk

CALCULATE YOUR INVESMENT OUTLOOK

Based on your investment outlook, you can select the stocks that tends to offer good returns. However the stock tends to generate better income over a period of 7-10 years.

GRASP THE BASICS

One need not be an expert but one should know the basics of trading in stock market. It consists of few core parameters of a company, such as company’s revenue, profits, debt profile, margin, and upcoming future growth prospectus. Moreover, it helps you to check company’s record on dividend payments. Check the stock performance in the last few years to know whether there is an increase in the prices. This could estimate about your future returns.

SAVE YOUR SAVINGS

While saving money might seem to be major drawback, it is easier than you think it. We just need a start. Estimate the monthly expenses and fix little amount that can be saved. . Even if you could save Rs 500-Rs1000 every month, at the end it will help you invest.

INITIATE SLOW

Investment is not a race. If you have rs.1000 to invest, look for the stocks that falls under your budget and find the best options. slowly and steadily, as your savings increase and your understanding of the market grows, you can have a handful of desired stocks based on your investor profile.

BE CAUTIOUS OF PENNY STOCKS:

The stocks that costs at just rs.10 or less are Penny stocks. All penny stocks aren’t bad. However, these stocks are low priced as they have low demand. It indicates that there are buyers for these stocks in trading market. The reason for the low cost can be due to company’s financial or it could be due company’s bankruptcy. Many investors go for penny stocks as they think it is the best option given limited resources. These seem to have a potential growth but these are the high-risk stocks and you should consider the risks involved in them carefully.

CAREFUL INVESTMENT

When investors start with little money, they tend to lose enthusiasm or delay many stocks for future since they are expensive. For instance, an IDFC Bank stock is priced at around Rs 1760 per share. If you have invested amount of Rs.1000, You can’t afford it. But if you have excess fund, say for ex: Rs 5000 as usual, you along with other investors in the same boat, tend to rush buying stocks such as IDFC Bank among others. Although the company might be fundamentally strong, investing in a round sum (lump sum) at wrong time can be ineffective. Any investment made today is expensive. Wait for the bind-up or perhaps, look for the good stock to invest in.

Expansion

Many novice investors think that expansion is for seasoned players. But this is not the case. Investors should consider expansion and don’t investment to one particular sector. If the entire sector was to suffer lose due to macro-economic reasons, the entire investment would be risky. Let’s see that in the year 2018 and 2019, Joe, new o stock market investment, had invested in Parma stocks. The stock came down due to multiple FDA problems across all companies. Resulting in the sector’s bad under-performance.a Luckily, he had invested in Hotel sector stocks too, which performed well. This had balanced his portfolio well.

Now, again in 2020, with the outbreak of pandemic, pharm stocks piled up well while the hotel stocks were down. His portfolio was in green signal thanks to the rise in pharm stocks. This is how diversifications multiply. It must be considered across investment instruments such as equities, debts, and mutual funds. Avoid Emotion-based investments resolutions: Many novice stock investors lose money as they allow their emotions to govern their buy and sell decisions. One should make decisions against facts and data and catch hold to quality stocks while selling away that are not strong fundamental enough.

YOU’RE STANCE

One of the other best option is to invest in Mutual Funds. Different mutual funds offer SIP, where you can invest the minimum of Rs.500 every month in equity funds. This gives an advantage to know the stock market without having to invest in high-priced stocks. Today many stock investment advice on the internet, with celebrity investors sharing their portfolios. New investors often imitate these portfolios thinking that they earn the same returns as earned by these celebrities. This is quite dangerous as many of the celebrities don’t share their entry or exit strategies. Therefore, stick to the basics and look for the best ways to begin with investment.
