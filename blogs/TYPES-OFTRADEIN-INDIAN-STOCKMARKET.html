WHAT ARE THE MAIN TYPES OF TRADE IN INDIAN STOCK MARKET?

Different types of trade in stock market offer an abundance of lucrative opportunities for traders, causing thousands of trading enthusiasts to jump into the stock market exchanges daily. While few are here to try their luck, as they think trading is purely a type of gambling, others make huge profits with their trading knowledge of trading tricks. In the following section, we’ll discuss the types of trade to make better choices and return in profits.

WHAT IS TRADING?

In general terms, trading refers to the exchange of goods or services between two individuals. In stock market, trading takes place between investors or traders and companies issuing stocks. The trading takes place in an organized manner with rules and regulations that must be adhere by everyone.

TYPES OF TRADING:

Traders use various types of trade strategies based on their market knowledge and conditions. One of the major drawbacks of using only one strategy is that the same strategy may not work for other. That’s the reason the organizations employ various techniques to trade in the market. With the help of advancement in the finance sector, the stock market has gone through several innovations giving the investors the better options to trade.

INTRADAY TRADING:

It’s a common stock market trading named as day trading. Though many traders use intraday strategy to make huge profits, it has involved huge risks too. Traders in this type purchase and sell the stocks the same day. Traders must eye-hawk on the momentum of ETF’s, indices, and stocks to place the orders at right time on the same day. Here, the trader can purchase the stocks in the beginning and sell later or opt for sell first and buy later strategy. Nevertheless, beginners must avoid trading on margin as it might increase their loss if the trade doesn’t pays back well.

POSITIONAL TRADING:

Positional trading is as same as intraday trading as it requires a continuous watch on monitor’s stock momentum before placing a buy order. Unfortunately, positional trading doesn’t allow for selling before buying. The traders use positional trading when they are looking for the long term gains while ignoring the short term fluctuations. Few traders evaluate the stock’s price action to notice their entry and exit points. Support and resistance lines are drawn on a chart to make them understand the time period of specific stock. Few traders use this type to predict a particular stock’s future direction.

SWING TRADING:

Swing traders analyze the stocks in different durations such as 5, 10, 15, 30, 60 minutes to 24 hours. It helps them locate the swings in price fluctuations. Occasionally swing trading may coincide with day or positional trading. It is one of the challenging types of trade due to high volatility and constant observation. Despite of the challenge, volatility is a swing’s best friend. The greater the volatility of stock, the better the outcome. Therefore, if a trader can accurately predict the swing trading, then it would result in best trading strategy.

LONG TERM TRADING

This is one of safest-long term strategy. It is suitable for conservative investors who don’t mind purchasing and holding stocks for years. The long term traders look for the insights of stock’s growth, balance sheets, reading news, acquiring industrial knowledge, and following economic updates.

SCALPING:

Scalping is similar to intraday trading. The intraday traders identify the opportunities for profits the whole day whereas scalping traders create several short-duration traders to effects the waves. Here, scalpers require high observation skills and experience to point traders and place orders. It’s common for scalpers to enter and exit multiple trades. It might last for just minutes to an hour.

MOMENTUM TRADING:

Momentum trading is one the simplest type of trading in stock market. Traders in this type may predict a stock’s movement to identify the correct time enter or exit. The optimal time to buy the stock is when the price is low and the appropriate time to exit is when a stock is expected to break out.

CONCLUSION:

Every trader has variable expectations from the money they invest in the market. It’s evident that they isn’t any best strategy to invest in the stock market. Remember success comes to those who identify their trading style to meet their specific requirements. The beginners must learn different types of trading to understand the things easier and identify their preferred techniques.
