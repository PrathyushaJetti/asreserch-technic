BASICS OF STOCK MARKET

BASICS OF STOCK MARKET

Every organization requires money to run their business. Normally the profits are acquired by selling goods and services may not be sufficient enough to meet the working capital requirements. Therefore, companies invite common people like us to invest some money into their company so that they can run it efficiently and in return, investors get profits for their shares.

Let’s learn the insights of initial stock market basics.

What are stocks?

Stocks are investment method to build wealth in a company. We own a share, when we invest in the stock of a company. Stock investment is a process of investing in some of the most successful companies.

There are various stocks available to invest/ trade-in. These stocks are classified on basis of following criteria:

  • Market Capitalization
  • Ownership
  • Price fluctuations
  • Profit sharing
  • Economic trends

What is Share Market?

People often assume that share market and stock market are same and use it interchangeably.

A Stock market is alike to a share market. A Share market is a place where shares are issued or traded in. The main difference between the two is that the stock market allows a trader to trade in bonds, mutual funds, derivatives, shares of a company, etc. Inversely, a share market only allows the trading of shares.

How Does the Stock Market Work?

Companies multiply their money on the stock market by selling ownership to investors. These stakes are known as shares of stock. By listing shares for sale on the stock market exchanges that makeup the stock market, companies get entry to the capital they need to operate and grow their businesses without taking on debt. Investors receive the profits by exchanging their money for shares on the stock market.

As companies invest that money to multiply and grow their businesses, it profits the investors as their shares become valuable in time, directing to capital gains. Additionally, companies pay dividend to their shareholders as their profits expands. The performance of every stock differ based on time but is taken it as a whole. The investors receive an average annual return of minimum 10% from the stock market, hence, making it most reliable way of growing your money.

Few important stock market terms:

A glossary list of most commonly used terms in stock market are given below:

Sensex: Sensex is a benchmark stock exchange comprising a collection of 30 stocks listed on the BSE market cap.

SEBI: Securities and Exchange of India is the market security regulator to look for the fraudulent transactions and illegal activity made by the party, companies, investors, traders, brokers, and other financial body.

Demat: It is a dematerialized account that holds the customer’s shares and securities in an electronic format.

Trading: A process of purchasing or selling shares in a company is termed as Trading.

Portfolio: It is a compilation of wide range of assets owned by investors. A portfolio includes gold, stocks, funds, derivatives, property, cash equivalents, bonds, etc.

Bear market: Bear market is a process of slowing down in the economy, which results in less consumer expense, and in turn, lower the GDP.

Nifty 50: A collection of top 50 companies recorded on the National Stock Exchange (NSE).

Stock Market Broker: An advisory person who sells or purchases the stocks on behalf of their clients.

Bid Price: The highest price the purchaser will pay to buy a specific number of shares of a stock at any given time.

Ask price: It refers to the price at which the seller will sell the stock in the stock market.

IPO: Initial Public Offer (IPO) is a process of selling securities to public in the primary market. It is the largest source of capital with long maturity for the company.

Equity: The value received by the shareholder when all the company’s assets were liquidated and all the debts were paid off, it is termed as equity.

Dividend: A dividend is a reward that a company gives to its shareholders in a form of cash payment, stocks or any other form.

BSE: Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) was established in 1875. It is headquartered in Mumbai India. It is also named as ‘The Native Share and Stock broker’. It is the biggest and first security exchange market in India providing equities trading platform for small and medium businesses.

NSE: According to World Federation of Exchanges (WFE) ,National Stock Exchange is the fourth largest stock exchange worldwide in terms of equity trading volume This is the first institution to implement screen-based trading in India.

Call & Put option: The call option gives investor the authority to buy the underlying security, whereas the put option gives the authority to sell the shares of the underlying security. Both opinions allow the investors to profit from momentum in a stock’s price.

Types of stock market: : Stock market is classified into two types: Primary Market: Primary market is a platform where firms float their new stock options and bonds for general public to obtain. Secondary market: The investor trade in securities with zero involvement of companies issued in the first place with the brokers assistance.

Ask and Close: The word ‘Ask’ refers to a seller who sells the stock at least price in the stock market. ‘Closing price’ refers to the last price of a stock trades during a regular trading session.

Moving average: It is used as a stock indicator for technical analysis to even out the price data by making a constant updated average price. A declining moving average shows a downtrend, while the rising moving average shows the uptrend security.
