EQUITY AND DERIVATIVES

Equity and derivatives are two financial instruments that are used for different purpose in the stock market. Derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from fundamental assets. Alternatively Equity represents the ownership, shareholders rights and responsibilities linked to the company’s performance.

KEY POINTS:

  • The main purpose of equity is increase in capital and ownership, whereas derivatives are helpful in hedging, speculation and leveraging.
  • Equity performance is affected by company and market trends, whereas derivatives strategies are based on current market conditions.
  • Equity defines simple ownership concept, whereas an insight is essential for derivatives due to its complexities in financial contracts.

WHAT IS EQUITY?

Equity is referred to stocks or shares, represents ownership in a company. When one possess the shares, you have an equity in a company; it denotes that you can claim on its assets and earnings. Some of the important points of equity are given below:

OWNERSHIP The equity buyer in a company will be a partial owner. The returns are linked to the company’s achievement that included profits and losses.

RISK AND REWARD It is combination of both high potential risk and high potential profits. Stock prices can vary depending on the market conditions and business performance.

DIVIDENDS The equity investors may receive a certain percentage of the company’s profits allocated to shareholders is termed as dividends. These can by a steady income line.

VOTING RIGHTS Based on the type of equity, you have the right to vote on particular company’s problems, such as the election of the board of directors, company’s policies etc.

WHAT ARE DERIVATIVES?

Equity is referred to stocks or shares, represents ownership in a company. When one possess the shares, you have an equity in a company; it denotes that you can claim on its assets and earnings. Some of the important points of equity are given below:

ZERO OWNERSHIP :Derivatives don’t give ownership in the underlying asset. Otherwise, they provide a way to engage on price movements without the asset itself.

LEVERAGE :Derivatives allow traders to use leverage; in simple words you can control a huge portion with a comparatively less amount of capital. With the profit, it can also increase the potential losses.

TYPES OF DERIVATIVES:Other than stocks and bonds, options and futures are also other derivatives.With options we have rights but not any responsibility to buy or sell an asset at a specific price.

RISK PROFILE:Derivatives are complex and can have certain risks. They justify the whole understanding of the market and the instrument itself.

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN EQUITY AND DERIVATIVES:

Derivatives are financial contracts. Its worth is determined by an underlying asset. These contacts are used in hedging, speculation, or Arbitrage. Some of the features of derivatives are as follows:

OWNERSHIP: Equity represents the ownership in an organization, whereas derivatives don’t grant ownerships.

PURPOSE: Equity is a long term holding time, whereas derivatives are short-term.

RISKS Even equity is subject to market risk, comparatively low risk than derivatives, whereas derivatives involve high risk complexities.

INCOME Based on the type of equity, you have the right to vote on particular company’s problems, such as the election of the board of directors, company’s policies etc.

VOTING RIGHTS: Provided maybe by dividends, whereas derivatives don’t provide income through dividends.

PROFIT TIMING Equity shareholders enjoy voting rights on Company’s matters, policies etc.; whereas derivatives have no voting rights.

Which is better option: Equity or Derivatives?

Whether short term or long term goals, by considering the below factors you can make an informed choice between equity and derivatives.

Opt for equity if you:
  • Pursue long term ownership and dividend income from a company.
  • You are comfortable with market fluctuations (volatility) and are ready to hold investments for a long time.
  • Want voting rights in company’s decision making.
  • Prefer simple investments with no complex financials contracts.
OPT for derivatives if you:
  • Are seasoned trader in financial market and comprehend the instrumental complexities.
  • Are comfortable with leverage and potential for profits and losses.
  • Know a specific strategy that involves goals, risk appetite, and comprehension of these financial instruments.

Ensure that the choice between equity and derivatives should align with your objectives, and understanding of these financial instruments.
